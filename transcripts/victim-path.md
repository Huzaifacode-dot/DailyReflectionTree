# Victim Path Example

### START

Good evening. Let’s reflect on your day.

---

### Q1: When something didn’t go your way today, what felt most true?

**Answer:** It was mostly bad timing or external stuff

---

### Q2: When a task didn’t go as expected, what was your first thought?

**Answer:** Things were out of my control

---

💡 *Reflection:*
It felt like a lot was outside your control today. Still, noticing small choices you made can shift how tomorrow unfolds.

---

### Q3: Think about one interaction today—what did it feel like from your side?

**Answer:** I felt like others weren’t pulling their weight

---

### Q4: At the end of the day, what stands out more to you?

**Answer:** The frustration of things not being fair

---

💡 *Reflection:*
It seems like fairness and recognition stood out to you. Shifting focus to what you can contribute may change that experience.

---

### Q5: When you think about today’s biggest moment, who does it feel like it was about?

**Answer:** Mostly me and my own work

---

💡 *Reflection:*
Your focus stayed close to your own role today. Expanding perspective can often reduce pressure.

---

### SUMMARY

You leaned **external in control**, **entitlement in contribution**, and **self-focused in perspective**.
